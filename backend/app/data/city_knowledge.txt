POLLUTION CONTROL AND ENVIRONMENTAL MONITORING

Air quality monitoring is a top priority for the city administration. We have deployed 180 IoT-enabled air quality sensors strategically located across all zones, including residential areas, industrial zones, high-traffic corridors, schools, and hospitals. These sensors continuously monitor six key pollutants: PM2.5 (fine particulate matter), PM10 (coarse particulate matter), NO2 (nitrogen dioxide), SO2 (sulfur dioxide), CO (carbon monoxide), and O3 (ground-level ozone).

Real-time Air Quality Index (AQI) data is updated every 15 minutes on the CitySense360 portal and mobile app. The AQI is categorized as: Good (0-50), Satisfactory (51-100), Moderate (101-200), Poor (201-300), Very Poor (301-400), and Severe (401-500). Color-coded maps show air quality variations across different areas of the city.

When air quality enters the "Poor" category or worse, automatic SMS and app notifications are sent to all registered citizens with health advisories. Schools and outdoor activity centers receive special alerts. During "Very Poor" or "Severe" conditions, restrictions may be imposed on construction activities, industrial operations, and vehicle movement.

Citizens can report pollution incidents through multiple channels:
1. CitySense360 Mobile App - "Report Pollution" feature with photo upload capability
2. City Portal Website - Online complaint form at pollution.citysense360.gov
3. Pollution Helpline - Call 1800-XXX-POLLUTION (available 24/7)
4. SMS Service - Send POLLUTION to 56677 with location details
5. Email - pollution.complaints@citysense360.gov

Common reportable pollution incidents include: industrial smoke emissions, vehicle exhaust violations, waste burning, sewage discharge into water bodies, noise pollution from construction or industries, and illegal dumping.

All pollution complaints are assigned a unique tracking ID. Citizens receive SMS confirmation within 1 hour, preliminary inspection within 24 hours, and resolution within 7 working days for standard complaints. Severe pollution incidents receive immediate attention with inspection teams deployed within 2 hours.

Industrial facilities are required to install Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems (CEMS) and submit monthly reports. Industries violating emission norms face penalties ranging from ₹50,000 to ₹10 lakh, and licenses may be suspended for repeated violations. The city conducts surprise inspections quarterly at all registered industrial units.

The Green City Initiative has established 35 urban forests and green belts covering 850 hectares. These areas serve as carbon sinks and improve local air quality. Citizens can volunteer for tree plantation drives organized on the first Sunday of every month. Over 200,000 trees have been planted in the last two years.

To combat vehicular pollution, the city offers substantial incentives for electric vehicle adoption:

- 100% discount on parking fees at public facilities
- Free charging at 75 government-operated charging stations

The city has 75 public EV charging stations (150+ charging points) strategically located at metro stations, shopping malls, office complexes, and residential hubs. Charging is free for the first year; subsequently, nominal rates apply (₹8-12 per unit). The ChargeEV app helps locate nearest charging stations with real-time availability.

Water quality monitoring covers 25 major water bodies including rivers, lakes, and reservoirs. Water samples are tested monthly for pH levels, dissolved oxygen, biochemical oxygen demand (BOD), and presence of heavy metals. Reports are published on the environment section of the city portal.

Noise pollution is regulated with strict limits: Residential areas (55 dB during day, 45 dB at night), Commercial areas (65 dB during day, 55 dB at night), Industrial areas (75 dB during day, 70 dB at night).
